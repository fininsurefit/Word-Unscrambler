How Much Should I Set Aside for Taxes as a 1099 Contractor?

Published: April 2026 • Tax Tips

As a 1099 contractor, you’re responsible for your own taxes—no one withholds them for you. A general rule is to set aside 25-35% of your income for federal, state, and self-employment taxes.

  • Self-Employment Tax (15.3%): This covers Social Security (12.4%) and Medicare (2.9%). Unlike W-2 employees, you pay the full amount.
  • Federal Income Tax: Your rate depends on your total income, filing status, and tax bracket.
  • State and Local Taxes: If your state has an income tax, you may need to set aside more.
  • Deductions & Write-Offs: The more business expenses you deduct (e.g., home office, mileage, equipment), the lower your taxable income.

Example Estimate

If you earn $50,000 per year as a freelancer, you might need to set aside $12,500 to $17,500 to cover taxes.

1099 vs. W-2: What's the Difference?

Published: March 2026 • Career Advice

Understanding the difference between being an independent contractor (1099) and an employee (W-2) is crucial for your finances and career stability.

Taxes Withheld

  • 1099 Contractor: No taxes are withheld. You must make quarterly estimated tax payments to avoid IRS penalties.
  • W-2 Employee: Federal, state, Social Security, and Medicare taxes are automatically deducted from each paycheck.

Benefits & Stability

  • 1099 Contractor: Offers flexibility and control over workload but lacks traditional benefits like health insurance, paid time off, or unemployment insurance.
  • W-2 Employee: Typically receives benefits like retirement plans, employer-sponsored health insurance, and labor law protections.

Which is Right for You?

If you prefer flexibility, tax deductions, and control, being a 1099 contractor may be better. If you value stability, benefits, and automatic withholding, a W-2 role might be the best option.

What Are the Different Types of 1099 Forms?

Published: Feb 2026 • Tax Forms

You might receive different types of 1099 forms depending on your income sources. Here are the most common ones:

  • 1099-NEC: For freelancers & independent contractors (most common).
  • 1099-K: Reports credit card & third-party payments (PayPal, Venmo, Stripe).
  • 1099-MISC: Used for miscellaneous income (awards, royalties, rent).
  • 1099-INT/DIV: Reports interest and dividends from investments.
  • 1099-G: Covers government payments (unemployment, tax refunds).
  • 1099-R: Reports retirement account distributions (IRAs, pensions).
